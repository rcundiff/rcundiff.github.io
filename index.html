Chronic Pain


Details

My thesis research with Dr. Anitescu, M.D., and Dr. Beckman, Ph.D., at the University of Chicago Pain Medicine Clinic seeks to determine how certain psychological conditions such as depression and alexithymia, in which a person is unable to recognize or express her own emotions, affect the progress of chronic pain treatment. We will analyze differences in pain and various psychological outcomes between those high or low in alexithymia.

Synesthesia


Details

I was awarded the Provost Fellowship for both the 2015-2016 academic year and the 2016 summer term for my research with Dr. Sutter, Ph.D., investigating the role of attention in grapheme-color synesthesia, a condition in which a letter or number elicits an experience of a particular color, regardless of the color of the type-print. We designed a number of experiments aimed at manipulating visual attention to produce differences in the synesthetic experience. The results of three experiments were presented at the Loyola University Chicago Undergraduate Research Symposium in April 2016 and April 2017.

Tattoos


Details

As a senior undergraduate, I worked on a research project which had begun as a class project with Dr. Davidson, Ph.D., exploring how tattoos affect women's body image, self-esteem, and perceived negative judgment. We created a survey available online to women who did and did not have tattoos. The results of this study will be presented at the International Conferences of Eating Disorders (ICED) in Chicago, April 2018.

High Energy Physics


Details

As a high school summer research intern in the High Energy Physics division, I assembled and soldered circuit boards used to test low-voltage power supplies destined for the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at CERN in Switzerland as part of Argonne’s ATLAS project. In addition, I wrote a research paper detailing the work being doing at the LHC and created a bill of materials for many other projects at Argonne.

About me
I am currently a Master’s student at the University of Chicago interested in researching and understanding chronic pain, with the goal of attaining a Ph.D. in neuroscience in the future. My love for research, my medical background, and my own experiences as a patient with chronic illness make me a unique candidate for this endeavor. When I’m not buried in my books, I love to watch Netflix (currently finishing The Crown and Peaky Blinders) and explore new restaurants around the city with my boyfriend. I dream of one day being a professor at a university to instill a love for neuroscience in students the way Dr. Sutter did for me, and to be the proud mother of at least five Basset Hounds.
